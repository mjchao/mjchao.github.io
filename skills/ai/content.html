I focused on artifical intelligence during my undergraduate studies. I found its results to be an additional "level" of magical beyond normal programs. For example, calculators were cool, but handwriting recognition was way more impressive. At the end of the day, I understood that much of it was overglorified (e.g. machine learning is pretty much just dressed-up optimization), but I enjoyed the applicability of the discipline and chose to study it in greater depth.

A lot of my work has been representing things (e.g. words, paragraphs, politicians) with embeddings and interpreting what the embeddings represent. The embeddings are always learned by neural networks, which tend to represent things that may be too subtle for humans to notice. One can make a lot of interesting discoveries from embeddings generated by neural networks.

I've also taken general artificial intelligence classes, so I'm familiar with a variety of standard methods and am comfortable reading papers on new ideas.
